Ever seen poker online or on TV and wondered how the probability percentages next to each hand are generated? If you're not a poker player, probably not, but here's an example from a notable hand in this year's 2017 World Series of Poker Main Event:

Given a number of players and their two-card starting hands, as well as (optionally) any community cards that have already been dealt, Poker50 simulates 100,000 trials and returns the percentage of trials won by each hand -- this approaches each hand's equity, or probability of winning the given hand, and is accurate up to 0.01%. Click Poker50 above to get started!

Instructions:
  • Type in an integer number of players between 2 and 9
  • Type in each of their hands with uppercase values, lowercase suits, and no spaces (Ace-King of hearts would be AhKh; a pair of tens, clubs and spades, would be TcTs)
  • Type in the board -- cards already dealt -- if any
  • Click run and view results!
